# ComputAI: A Drug Repurposing Discovery App

**ComputAI** is an AI-powered computational biology assistant built on [PartyRock (AWS)](https://partyrock.aws) that generates research-grade hypotheses for early-stage drug discovery and drug repurposing. Given a candidate compound and a biological target, it produces structured scientific reports covering binding mechanisms, molecular pathways, repurposing potential, and predicted safety profiles.

> Try the app: [ComputAI on PartyRock](https://partyrock.aws/u/Only1Brenna/61fhzWBKy/ComputAI%253A-A-Drug-Repurposing-Discovery-App)

---

## What It Does

ComputAI takes two inputs:

| Input | Description |
|---|---|
| **Candidate Compound** | A drug name, natural compound, or substance (e.g., Metformin, quercetin, garlic extract) |
| **Target Receptor** | A protein or receptor name (e.g., EGFR, ACE2, mTOR) |

It then generates two parallel AI reports using **Claude Sonnet 4.6**:

---

### Report 1: Binding Mechanism Hypothesis and Pathway Analysis

A structural pharmacology report covering:

- **Binding Mechanism Hypothesis** — Predicted binding mode (competitive inhibition, allosteric modulation, covalent binding, etc.) and key residues involved in ligand coordination
- **Structural Binding Clues** — How the compound's pharmacophoric elements complement the target's active site topology and conserved motifs
- **Molecular Pathway Involvement** — Primary signaling or metabolic pathways modulated (e.g., MAPK, PI3K-AKT, JAK-STAT), including upstream/downstream effectors and anticipated cellular phenotype
- **Selectivity Considerations** — Off-target proteins with structural homology that may be inadvertently engaged, and implications for the selectivity window

---

### Report 2: Drug Repurposing and Safety Profile Report

A translational medicine and computational toxicology report covering:

- **Repurposing Potential** — Novel therapeutic indications beyond the compound's approved use, with justification linking target biology to disease pathophysiology (oncology, metabolic disease, neurodegeneration, infectious disease, autoimmunity)
- **Polypharmacology Opportunities** — Multi-target engagement strategies and combination therapy hypotheses
- **Predicted Toxicity and Side-Effect Profile** — SAR-based adverse effect predictions categorized by organ system (hepatotoxicity, cardiotoxicity, CNS effects, etc.) with qualitative risk levels
- **ADMET Considerations** — Absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, and toxicity properties, including CYP450 interactions, plasma protein binding, and blood-brain barrier penetration relevance
- **Regulatory and Development Outlook** — Clinical development stage, existing safety data, and feasibility of an IND or repurposing clinical trial

## Motivation

Thousands of FDA-approved drugs have untapped therapeutic potential beyond their original indication. ComputAI was built to accelerate the early ideation phase of drug repurposing research — not just for approved pharmaceuticals, but also for naturally occurring compounds like plant-derived substances, nutraceuticals, and bioactive molecules.

The goal is to give students and researchers a fast, structured starting point for hypothesis generation before committing to wet lab experiments.

## Intended Audience

- Undergraduate and graduate biology, pharmacology, and biochemistry students
- Early-stage drug discovery researchers
- Computational biology enthusiasts exploring repurposing hypotheses

## Example Use Cases

- Query **Metformin** against **mTORC1** to explore its anti-aging or oncology repurposing potential
- Query **Quercetin** against **ACE2** to investigate natural compound antiviral hypotheses
- Query **Aspirin** against **COX-2** to review its established mechanism and explore novel indications
- Query **Curcumin** against **NF-kB** to evaluate anti-inflammatory pathway involvement

---

## Disclaimer

All outputs generated by ComputAI are AI-synthesized research summaries intended for **educational and early-stage exploratory use only**. They do not constitute medical advice, clinical guidance, or validated experimental results. Additional laboratory and clinical research is necessary to evaluate any hypothesis generated by this tool.
